Reflections

These reflections highlight what I’m learning as I develop skills in instructional design and educational technology—what worked, what I’d change, and how I’m applying research-informed practices to create accessible, engaging learning experiences.

Designing for Accessibility First

I’ve adopted an accessibility-first mindset. For each page and activity, I walk through a quick checklist that includes descriptive titles and logical heading order, sufficient color contrast, meaningful alt text, keyboard navigability with visible focus states, and clear link styles and microcopy.
