Mental Health Interventions for Adolescents Raised by Grandparents

Course: Research Methods in Information Science and Learning Technologies (ISLT 9450)

Date: April 2025

This project investigates the unique mental health challenges of adolescents being raised by grandparents due to parental incarceration and/or substance use. Using an explanatory sequential mixed-methods approach, I first analyzed Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ) data, followed by qualitative interviews to better understand the lived experiences of these youth. The findings highlight emotional, behavioral, and academic impacts, as well as systemic barriers to care.

Key Insights

  • Adolescents face heightened emotional and behavioral risks compared to peers.
  • Grandparents experience strain from custody battles, financial stress, and limited support networks.
  • Schools are a crucial point of intervention, but resources remain inconsistent.
  • Phenomenology and Resilience Theory guided the framework for exploring lived experiences and coping mechanisms.

Proposed Interventions

  • School-based counseling and mentoring programs
  • Community partnerships to expand resource access
  • Therapeutic approaches, such as Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
  • Policy-level advocacy for legal and financial supports to kinship caregivers
