Mental Health Interventions for Adolescents Raised by Grandparents

Course: Research Methods in Information Science and Learning Technologies (ISLT 9450)

Date: April 2025

This study explores how being raised by grandparents due to parental incarceration or substance use impacts adolescents aged 11–17 in Southeast Missouri. It applies an explanatory sequential mixed-methods design, beginning with quantitative SDQ mental health scores followed by qualitative interviews to uncover lived experiences.

Highlights

  • Identified emotional, behavioral, and academic challenges for adolescents in grandparent-led households.
  • Explored systemic barriers for grandparents, including custody, financial strain, and access to mental health care.
  • Applied Phenomenology and Resilience Theory as guiding frameworks.
  • Proposed school-based interventions, community resources, and structured therapies (e.g., ACT).
